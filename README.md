# Hi there, I'm Luiz F. D. Sarmento 👋

Computational biologist. Using **single-cell transcriptomics** and **immune repertoire data** to study **inflammation** and **cancer immunology**.

I work on **reproducible bioinformatics pipelines** (R/Python) that connect what cells are doing at the molecular level (scRNA-seq) with how B cells and antibodies evolve (scBCR-seq), with a focus on **B-cell maturation**, **somatic hypermutation (SHM)**, **clonal dynamics**, and **tertiary lymphoid structures (TLS)** in tumors.

My research interests sit at the intersection of **tumor immunology**, **single-cell genomics**, and **systems-level analysis**—including cell-state annotation, trajectory inference, cell–cell communication, and integrating repertoire + transcriptome signals to generate testable hypotheses.

In other words, I study data that helps explain *what immune cells are doing*, *how B-cell responses mature*, and *why some tumors respond differently to immunotherapy*.
